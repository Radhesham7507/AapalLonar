Kamalja Devi Temple

Kamalja Devi Temple, Lonar Located on the banks of Lonar Lake, Kamalja Devi Temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kamalja, also known as Padmavati. According to legend, the Goddess appeared Swayambhu (self-manifested) here.

Temple Description The temple is said to be over 5th century old and is considered one of the oldest temples in the region. Although it is in a dilapidated condition, it still maintains its historical significance. The temple is surrounded by Lonar Lake, adding to its serene and peaceful atmosphere.

Goddess Kamalja Goddess Kamalja is believed to be an avatar of Goddess Lakshmi. According to mythology, Lord Vishnu fought and killed a demon named Lavanasur (salt-demon) here, and Goddess Kamalja is said to have emerged from the earth.

Visiting Information Visitors can visit the temple from dawn to dusk, and photography and video recording are allowed. However, it is essential to maintain the cleanliness of the temple and its surroundings.

Note Please refrain from spreading litter or damaging the temple’s structure to preserve its historical significance and beauty.
